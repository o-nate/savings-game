Maximizing Your Final {{ Lexicon.savings_account }} Balance

Maximizing the final balance of your {{ Lexicon.savings_account }} during the game requires a strategy of protecting the {{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }} of your {{ Lexicon.savings_account }}. Below, we explain how to protect your {{ Lexicon.savings_account }} {{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }} and how to use the information displayed on the game screen to do so.

{{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }}

In the game, {{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }} refers to the amount of {{ Lexicon.food }} you can buy with your {{ Lexicon.total_cash }}. When the price of {{Lexicon.food}} increases, your {{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }} decreases. On the other hand, earning interest on your {{ Lexicon.savings_account }} increases the {{Lexicon.purchasing_power}} since you have more {{ Lexicon.total_cash }} and can buy more {{ Lexicon.food }} (price being equal).

{{ Lexicon.inflation_rate }}

The percentage by which the price of {{ Lexicon.food }} changes is the {{ Lexicon.inflation_rate }}. If prices increase, this means the {{ Lexicon.inflation_rate }} is positive. When price increases accelerate — increasing by more and more each month — this is because the {{ Lexicon.inflation_rate }} is increasing.

{{ Lexicon.interest_rate }}

At the end of each month, the balance in your {{ Lexicon.savings_account }} increases by {{ nominal_interest_rate }}%. This percentage is the {{ Lexicon.interest_rate }}. When the price of {{ Lexicon.food }} does not change, the {{ Lexicon.purchasing_power }} of your {{Lexicon.savings_account}} increases by {{ nominal_interest_rate }}%.
